Alan Turing

The father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London
  • 1934 - Graduated at King's College, Cambridge, with a degree in mathematics
  • 1935 - At age 22, was elected a Fellow of King's College on the strength of a dissertation in which he proved the central limit theorem.
  • 1936 - Published his "On Computable Numbers" paper, introducing the concept of the Turing Machine and the Turing Test
  • 1938 - Obtained his PhD from the Department of Mathematics at Princeton University
  • 1938 - During WW2, Worked for the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS) at Bletchley Park, Britain's codebreaking centre that produced Ultra intelligence and helped break Germany's Enigma Code.
  • 1945 - Worked at the National Physical Laboratory, where he designed the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE), one of the first designs for a stored-program computer
  • 1948 - Joined Max Newman's Computing Machine Laboratory, at the Victoria University of Manchester, where he helped develop the Manchester computers

  • 1952 - Prosecuted for homosexual acts (then a crime)
  • 1954 - Died by cyanide poisoning. An inquest determined his death as a suicide, but it has been noted that the known evidence is also consistent with accidental poisoning.
